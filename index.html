Hello, I’m Fabien Basmaison!

As a Frontend developer, I’ve always pushed for Web Standards, resource optimization and fluid designs. I continue now with Responsive Web Design and like to fiddle with SVG.

As a former architect, I also have a sense for graphics and structure, and a fair understanding of how people interact with objects.

As a traveller, I like the idea of working remotely, be it close to the office, or out in the wild.

Some projects I worked on

pompage.net

2004 ~ 2014: http://www.pompage.net/about I didn’t design the website but I was maintaining it on my spare time, along with translating, proof‑reading and publishing. Being part of this team for 10 years has been awesome and set the path I am still following to this day.

paris-web.fr

2006 ~ 2009: As part of Paris Web team, I participated to the organization and integration of the related websites. The whole process was fun! Helping speakers before, during and after the event was one of the most rewarding experiences ever. It was also a nice introduction to working remotely, with people in France, New‑Caledonia and China.

bodybag.me

2009: http://bodybag.me/ is a website for two Dutch architects in Shanghai who started their fashion design company.

flipmood.com

2010: http://flipmood.com/ was a website for friends that wanted to promote their creative works. Working with these two was amazingly insightful about what can be expected from good graphic designers.

china-europa-forum.net

2010: http://china-europa-forum.net/ is an initiative to push for exchange and support between Europe and China, on a lot of different topics. I coded the frontend and CMS of the site at the time; the actual version saw some HTML content added by a third part which can break the layout.

skyview.asia

2011: http://www.skyview.asia/ was a command from a friend. The concept was simplicity and fullscreen.

poppen.de

2010 ~ 2012: (NSFW) http://poppen.de/ is part of what my previous company is doing. There’s more than 3 millions registered users which makes it a very interesting challenge as any change made has a real impact.

kaufmich.com

2012~2014: (NSFW) On http://kaufmich.com/, my biggest challenge was to clean legacy code while adding more features to the website. Considering the amount of people and code added through the years, it was quite some fun!

Using grunt, sass and a PHP version of KSS, I started the HTML & CSS integration of a mobile first version based on RWD before I left in September. This was the first stone of multiple goals:

  • To revamp the whole website by gradually moving the desktop features to a RWD version presented as the mobile version, then switching the desktop to the RWD website.
  • To improve collaboration between graphic designer, marketing, support and development teams by providing live style‑guides for reference.

juwai.com

2015~2016: http://juwai.com/ is the main website of a few, dedicated to Chinese consumers and customers throughout the World.

I worked on almost every frontend related project, from the administrative side of applications, to a global redesign of www.juwai.com that started in December 2015.
